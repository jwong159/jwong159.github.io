Homepage

David Byrne is a remarkable, multifaceted artist. Born in Scotland in 1952, Byrne holds British Scottish, Irish and United States citizenship. In 1976, Byrne, Chris Frantz and Tina Weymouth formed Talking Heads. Multi-instrumentalist Jerry Harrison joined the band in 1977 after they signed with Sire Records. Throughout the double-platinum success of the band, Byrne has released solo and collaborative work with the likes of Brian Eno and St. Vincent. He is also a visual and conceptual artist shown throughout the world. Moreover, Byrne has scored award-winning films ("The Last Emperor" with Ryuichi Sakamoto and Cong Su), cowritten musicals ("Here Lies Love" with Fatboy Slim) and movie theme songs ("This Is a Life" with Mitski, featured on the soundtrack of "Everything Everywhere All at Once)."
